# Figure 1 caption (draft, descriptive)

**Figure 1.** Study area in the western Great Lakes region of the United States, spanning Wisconsin,
Minnesota, and the Michigan Upper Peninsula, rendered in EPSG:5070 (CONUS Albers, equal-area). The
map shows the study grid extent in grey, the Great Lakes, and the seven National Park Service Great
Lakes Inventory and Monitoring Network (GLKN) park units used in this analysis, each outlined in a
distinct color and named in the legend: Apostle Islands National Lakeshore, Grand Portage National
Monument, Isle Royale National Park, Mississippi National River and Recreation Area, Saint Croix
National Scenic Riverway, Sleeping Bear Dunes National Lakeshore, and Voyageurs National Park. The
180 interpreted reference cells are marked as black squares. The locator inset shows the region
within the conterminous United States, with the red box marking the extent of the main map. The scale
bar is in kilometers and north is up. State, province, and Great Lakes boundaries are from Natural
Earth (1:50 million); the study grid, the GLKN watershed boundaries, and the interpreted-cell list
are exported from Google Earth Engine.
